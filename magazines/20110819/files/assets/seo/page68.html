By John Moorehouse, Speedhorse Staff

Jess A Runner wasn’t just another competitor in the Great Lakes Stakes. Not only did he win the $17,150 stakes on July 24 at Canterbury Park, he broke a seven-year-old track record at 440-yards, covering the distance in 21.162 seconds and registering a scorching 119 speed index. Capones Vault held the previous record, a 21.695-second run set on July 3, 2004. Kenny Laymon trains Jess A Runner, and shook up the five-year-old gelding’s regimen heading into the Great Lakes by swimming him on a regular basis. It apparently worked.

After breaking second out of the four-hole, Jess A Runner grabbed a one-length lead at first call. Jockey Doug Frink brought the gelding into the stretch enjoying a

1 1/2-length cushion. That, however, apparently wasn’t enough for the gelding, who bolted away from the rest of the field to win by a commanding three-lengths. Owned by JK Running Horses LLC, Jess A Runner now has seven wins in 21 career outs. His lifetime earnings increased to $142,374 with this $8,575 addition. That includes three victories in stakes company. Jess A Runner finished first in a pair of graded stakes at Prairie Meadows last season.

Paul Newmaster bred Jess A Runner out of the 1999 mare Katiebug Driver, whom he also owns and bred. Of the five starters produced by Katiebug Driver, two hold black type. Pretty Girl Kate, a half-sister to Jess A Runner, won the 2007 Kansas Bred Derby.

It was a very fast track for the Great

Lakes, with four of the top five finishers posting a speed index above 100. Streakin Rare (Fredricksburg-Regally Rare) took second place for owner/breeder Michael Teel. The Charley Hunt trainee had jockey Jennifer Schmidt aboard. Behavin Cartel (Southern Cartel-Love Miss Behavin) placed third. The filly is owned by Casey Foster, trained by Robert Johnson and jockey Clyde Smith drew the riding assignment.

Value The Man (Heza Fast Man-Sheeza Lil Val) and Hard To Ketch (Fast Debonair-Rockin Storm TB) completed the field.
