He gained a one-length comfort zone in the early going and held it over the wire. He earned $38,393 for a career total of $244,647. He’s won fve-in-a-row, including two graded stakes. Earlier in the year he was second in the Oklahoma Derby. His lifetime record is 6-2-1 from 12 with a 108 speed index. He’ll rest until the trials for the Texas Classic Derby at Lone Star. Sills’ name is a familiar one in stakes circles. He breeds approximately 50 percent of his runners and buys the other 50 percent. “Sure, it’s especially satisfying

to breed a winner,” he said. “You know how it goes; you have a little mare who can run some. You fall in love with her and she has a home for the rest of her life. You can’t let her just stand there. You gotta’ do something with her, so you breed her.”

That’s the way a broodmare band grows and grows, one love story at a time. Sills and Willis waited and now all those good things are going their way.

Capo De Capi is the frst foal out of the 2004 A Shot At Glory, bred by the Allred Brothers and owned by Bobby Cox. She has two foals from 2009 and one from 2010 who haven’t started.
