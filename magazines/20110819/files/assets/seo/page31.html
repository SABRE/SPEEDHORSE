Speedhorse Gold and Silver Cup Derby-G3 Capo DE Capi

By Diane M. Ciarloni

Most of us have heard the adage that tells us, “All good things come to those who wait.” If anyone doubts the veracity of those words, just take a close look at Capo De Capi.

Bred by P.K. Thomas/Kim Kubinski and owned by James Sills, the three-year-old bay colt never ran a really terrible race as a freshman but neither was he a headline-maker. He earned only $42,783 from fve starts but the numbers never discouraged Sills. And trainer Eddie Willis never doubted the talent he felt he spotted when the colt walked into the Heritage Place sale ring. “I wasn’t even at the sale,” said Sills. “I trust Eddie completely and he was the one who bought the colt. We always knew he could run, that he had the speed, but things just worked against him last year.”

The most signifcant thing working against him was his shins. They sored up early and he could never shake the problem. He

gave all he could in his fve freshman starts but those shins kept him short of 100 percent. Capo De Capi’s fnal start last year was on Halloween at Will Rogers Downs in a futurity trial. He was sixth. Willis broke training for the colt and gave him more than four months of R and R. What happened during that hiatus was amazing. The colt’s frst effort in 2011

was March 6 in trials for the Oklahoma Derby. He fnished third, qualifed, and was second in the fnals. He looked good and there was no trouble from his shins. Willis tweaked and tuned on the colt and then sent him out on April Fool’s Day in a 300-yard allowance.
