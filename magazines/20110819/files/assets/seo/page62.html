By John Moorehouse, Speedhorse Staff

Odds-makers and those placing wagers thought so highly of Denver Pass that the colt was the favorite at post time for the Rainbow Juvenile Stakes. By the time the $20,000 event concluded, Denver Pass had met those expectations—just barely. After breaking out at the start of the 400-yard race, Denver Pass had to make a late surge and beat longshot Escort West to the wire by a head to claim his second win in three career outs and his first in stakes company.

Home-bred by owners Weetona Stanley and William Smith, Denver Pass has yet to finish worse than third in any of his starts. That one third-place finish came in the Rainbow Futurity trials. Though the colt didn’t qualify for the finals, he did enter the Rainbow Juvenile as the 5-2 favorite. Saddled by trainer G. Blane Wood, Denver Pass emerged from the four-gate running fourth. Despite breaking wide, the

colt remained third both at first call and as he went into the stretch. The colt shifted into a strong surge and, as he did, then-leader Viva Arriba faded. There was plenty of running room for Denver Pass to reach the wire first by a head.

Jockey Jacky Martin was aboard for the stakes-winning trip, completed in 19.899 seconds for an 87 speed index.

Next up for Denver Pass is trials for the All American Futurity.

Denver Pass joins his dam, First Carolina, in black type. The 2004 mare claimed two stakes wins during a career

that brought in more than $400,000 in prize money. Stanley and Smith also own and bred the mare, who has produced two starters out of three total offspring.

Escort West (Fly Jess Fly-Chain Of Fools) had a bumpy ride but still finished second. Dream Walkin Farms owns the gelding,

trained by Luis Villafranco for jockey Gaspar Garcia. Lotta Love For Robyn (First Down Dash-Love Ya Miss Jess) took third by a nose. Wayne Barton is the owner and the John Bassett trainee had jockey Saul Ramirez Jr. in the irons. The remaining order of finish was Viva Arriba (PYC Paint Your Wagon-Arriba Reba), Priceless Feature (Feature Mr Jess-Southern Price), Mighty Famous Vic (Mighty Invictus-Fame Us Katie), Stolis Rogue (Stoli-Hug A Rogue) and Cadillac Cartel (Corona Cartel-Jessie Rae). Feature Zoe and VGC Moon Walk scratched.
