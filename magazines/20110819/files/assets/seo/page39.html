Errata

Norman, OK—The breeders of Little Bit Trashy, who appeared on page 20 of our July 15 issue after setting a new track record winning the $458,180 Grade 1 Lee Berwick Memorial Futurity at Delta Downs on July 3, are Wade and Kay Loup.

Heat Forces Prairie Meadows To Cancel Racing

Norman, OK—The heat wave across the Midwest forced Prairie Meadows to cancel races shortly after the first race was run on July 19 after jockeys voted not to ride. The riders said the 97-degree temperature and 111-degree heat index made conditions hazardous to the horses. The Iowa track’s Thoroughbred meet runs through August 13, and the Quarter Horse meet is set to begin August 19 and to run through October 1.

High Temperatures Causing Hardship Norman, OK—The recent 100 plus temperatures and drought conditions in many states across the Midwest have put additional stress on horse and cattle owners. Pasture for grazing has dried up and hay is hard to find. Oklahoma’s Secretary of Agriculture is asking people with hay to register with the state so individuals can go to the directory on their website to locate hay. Many horse owners and ranchers have not been able to feed their animals, resulting in an increased number of horses and cattle at sales going for lower prices.

Retama Park Increases Purses

Norman, OK—Retama Park in Selma, Texas, has raised purses an average of $1,000 per race due to a significant increase in handle and attendance. Live handle is up 11% over 2008, the last time Retama conducted a live Quarter Horse meet, and export handle is up 70% compared to the same time.

Arapahoe Park Increases Purses Norman, OK—With increases in attendance and wagering handle, Arapahoe Park in Aurora, Colorado, raised purses for the final three weeks of racing. Purses for all overnight races were increased by $1,500, and all trials were increased by $500.

Los Alamitos On Groupon

Norman, OK—A Night at the Races at Los Alamitos was the Deal of the Day on Groupon.com on July 30-31, with the package featuring a total value of $81,50 for the purchase of only $35. The deal included two Vessels Club passes, preferred parking, winner’s circle photograph, $10 betting voucher, $10 food voucher, racing program, and two Los Alamitos logo shot glasses.

Canterbury Park Inducts Hall Of Fame Members

Norman, OK—Canterbury Park on August 6 inducted their 2011 Hall of Fame members. Included were Quarter Horse trainer Ed Ross Hardy, jockey Derek Bell, TB breeders Jeff and Deb Hilger, TB trainer Mac Robertson, and Minnesota-Bred Thoroughbred Sir Tricky.

Rock To Ride Concert At The Red Mile Race Track

Norman, OK—A Rock To Ride concert will be held Saturday, August 27 in Paddock Park at the Red Mile Race Track in Lexington, Kentucky, to raise funds and awareness for Horse Aid Live, a charitable fundraising organization that provides a way to give to one entity which then distributes annual grants to other recognized organizations that retrain, retire, rehabilitate, or shelter equines of all breeds throughout the U.S. Horse Aid Live is supported by the American Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals (ASPCA), American Horse Council, Kentucky Equine Research, the Unwanted Horse Coalition, and many other organizations.

Hendra Horse Deaths Reach 13 In Australia Norman, OK—Thirteen horses have died of the Hendra virus since June in Queensland and Northern New South Wales in Australia. The latest death from the bat-borne virus suggests the horse may have had a previous Hendra infection and developed antibodies. The case reinforces the suspicion that the virus can lie dormant in horses and people, and then emerge long after the initial infection.
