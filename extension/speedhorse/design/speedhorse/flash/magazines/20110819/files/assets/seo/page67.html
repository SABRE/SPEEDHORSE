By John Moorehouse, Speedhorse Staff

Lien On Me’s connections enjoyed a surprising result in the $18,100 Bob Morehouse Memorial/Cash Caravan Stakes on July 24 at Canterbury Park. Facing 8-1 odds, the three-year-old gelding made a late move to win the event, posting his third victory in nine career outs and his first in stakes company. Lien On Me already was stakes-placed, finishing third in last year’s Minnesota Futurity and second earlier this season in the Minnesota Stallion Breeders’ Derby. Canterbury Re-Opens

Norman, OK—Canterbury Park re-opened live racing, simulcast and card casino operations July 21 after a 20-day suspension due to a budget impasse that led to a partial government shutdown. The stalemate ended July 20 and employees of the Minnesota Racing Commission returned to work. During the suspension, 1,000 employees of Canterbury Park were laid off without pay, not to mention the hardship to countless individuals with jobs in the racing, hospitality and equine industries as well as to the many horsemen ready to compete at the Shakopee track. It is estimated that Canterbury Park lost as much as $1 million in revenue each week.
