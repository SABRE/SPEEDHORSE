By John Moorehouse, Speedhorse Staff

Snip N Dale continues to cut a successful swath through the Pacific Northwest. The six-year-old gelding claimed his sixth stakes victory, winning the $15,500 Jack Rhoden Memorial Handicap on July 17 at Crooked River Roundup. Five of Snip N Dale’s stakes triumphs are registered in the state of Oregon, including the past two versions of the Jack Rhoden Handicap. The overwhelming favorite at the betting window, Snip N Dale broke from the two-gate running third but held the advantage at each remaining call of the 250-yard sprint. The gelding led L Bar D Snow Princes by a head going into the stretch, then widened the gap to reach the wire with a 1 1/2-length separation. Prepped by trainer Scott Raley, Snip N Dale covered the 250-yards in 13.270 seconds, exactly matching his time from the 2010 Jack Rhoden, which set an NTR at that

distance. It’s one of five records the gelding holds. He’s logged 22 wins in 31 outs, with only two races out of the top three. Snip N Dale had jockey Nikeela Black aboard, and brought home a $7,500 prize for owner/breeder Gary Chumbley, increasing his lifetime bankroll to $183,114.

Chumbley also owns Bedaub Adash, the 1996 dam of Snip N Dale. Half of her eight starters from 10 foals have black type. While Snip N Dale is the most successful offspring, half-sister Two Dash To Fame, full-sister Lightnin Storm Cloud and half-brother

Cupids Dash also hold at least one stakes win. Two Dash To Fame and Cupids Dash share the same sire, Dash Ta Fame.

L Bar D Snow Princes beat RR Red Rocket to the wire by a neck to finish second. Darrell and Linda Sanders own

L Bar D Snow Princes (This Snow Is Royal-Dash Home Deb), trained by Robert Beckner for jockey H. Herrera. RR Red Rocket (Southern Cartel-Lanty Bug) is owned and trained by Robert Raley, and had jockey Terrance Birdrattler aboard. Hannibal Lector (Fastnfierce-Foxy Speeder), Iza All Dat N More (Juno Dat Cash-Ric O Chet Dundee), Eyes Okey (Okey Dokey Dale-All Eyes On Madison) and Count Alleia (Count Corona-Dixie Alleia) rounded out the order of finish.

Texas A&M Receives $2.5 Million Gift From Burnett Foundation In Honor Of Dr. Glenn Blodgett

Norman, OK—The Burnett Foundation awarded a $2.5 million challenge grant to the Texas A&M Foundation for the Equine Initiative in honor of Dr. Glenn Blodgett, a leader in equine veterinary medicine and a 2011 College of Veterinary Medicine Distinguished Alumnus. The grant establishes the Glenn Blodgett Equine Chair, and will help establish one of the premier equine programs in the nation by supporting the Equine Initiative to improve the equine industry and welfare of the horse. The Equine Initiative is built on four imperatives; curriculum enhancement, outreach and engagement expansion, facility construction and partnership development. It is a collaboration between Texas A&M’s College of Veterinary Medicine & Biomedical Sciences (CVM) and College of Agriculture & Life Science Department of Animal Science.

Dr. Glenn Blodgett is a 1974 graduate of the CVM, and has worked as a large animal veterinarian for over 35 years. He has been the resident veterinarian and horse division manager for Burnett Ranches, LLC (6666 Ranch) in Guthrie, Texas, since 1982. Under his leadership, Burnett Ranches has earned numerous awards and distinctions from the American Quarter Horse Association. Dr. Blodgett has contributed to the horse industry in many areas, particularly in equine embryo transfer and artifcial insemination.
